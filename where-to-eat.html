Dining in Schönfeld

Two restaurants. One legendary street. A culinary experience you won't forget.

The Dorfstraße Dining Mile

Both of Schönfeld's restaurants are located on the Dorfstraße — making it, per capita, one of the most restaurant-dense streets in rural Germany. With two establishments serving 675 residents, Schönfeld achieves a restaurant-to-resident ratio of 1:337.5 — surpassing many major European capitals.

What the Dorfstraße lacks in Michelin stars, it makes up for in authenticity, homemade Klöße, and the genuine possibility that the chef will ask about your family.

Gasthof Hahner

Franconian Farm-to-Table

The crown jewel of the Dorfstraße. Gasthof Hahner doesn't just serve Franconian cuisine — they grow it. The Kroketten and Klöße are made from self-grown potatoes. The meat comes from their own farm and butchering operation. The term "farm-to-table" is not a marketing concept here — it's a walking distance of approximately 12 meters.

The atmosphere is rustic, the portions are generous, and the freshness of ingredients is scientifically impossible to surpass without eating the potato directly from the ground (which we do not recommend, but respect).

Gasthaus "Zum Hofjäger"

Traditional German Gasthaus

"The Court Hunter" — a name that evokes images of aristocratic hunting parties returning to a roaring fire with tales of conquest. The reality is a cozy Gasthaus on the Dorfstraße where Anita Krapf serves traditional German cuisine with an outdoor seating area that, on warm evenings, constitutes approximately 40% of Schönfeld's nightlife.

The Hofjäger represents the classic German Gasthaus tradition: unpretentious, reliable, and the kind of place where everybody knows your name — statistically inevitable in a village of 675.

📍 Dorfstraße 15, 97950 Großrinderfeld-Schönfeld 📞 09344 285 👤 Operated by Anita Krapf 🌳 Outdoor seating available 💳 Cash preferred

Taubertal Wine Country

1,100 hectares of liquid heritage

Schönfeld sits at the heart of the "Liebliches Taubertal" wine region — 1,100 hectares of vineyards producing wines that range from excellent to "I should buy a case."

Key varieties include Müller-Thurgau (the workhorse), Silvaner (the intellectual), Kerner and Bacchus (the crowd-pleasers), and the legendary Tauberschwarz — a historic local red grape variety so rare, it makes Romanée-Conti look mass-produced.

The nearby village of Beckstein (15 km) offers a wine education path through vineyards and hosts the third-oldest wine cooperative of the Baden Wine Association. The 204 km Taubertal Wine Road connects numerous cellars, tastings, and festivals.
