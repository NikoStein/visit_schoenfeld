Things To Do

An exhaustive guide to Schönfeld's world-class attractions

Schloss Schönfeld

Late-Gothic grandeur since 1376

Two buildings. One monument protection status. Centuries of noble families whose names have been largely forgotten by everyone except the authors of this website.

Also known as the "Schönefelder Schlösschen" (the Schönfeld Little Castle — a name that manages to be both endearing and slightly patronizing), this Late-Gothic complex sits at 305 meters elevation and is privately owned. Visits are best conducted from the outside, where imagination can do the heavy lifting.

Notable former residents: A Seldeneck (~1520), Caspar Lerch von Dirmstein (~1620), Daniel von Rieneck (~1641). These names meant something once. In Schönfeld, they still do.

St. Vitus Cathedral

We use the term "cathedral" with the confidence that comes from being a tourism website. Technically, it's the Pfarrkirche St. Vitus (Parish Church of St. Vitus), but when your architect creates a modernist concrete-and-shell-limestone monument that was consecrated by Archbishop Hermann Schäufele himself on June 30, 1974, you've earned a little hyperbole.

Architectural Details

Built: 1971–1974 (replacing the 1827 church, which replaced the 1344 church, which was destroyed in the Thirty Years' War)

Architect: Manfred Schmitt-Fiebig

Materials: Concrete and chiseled shell limestone blocks

Style: Modernist brutalism meets Franconian piety

Tower: Original from 1827/28 — survived the demolition of the old church. A survivor.

Bells: Four-bell system in the tower

Art & Treasures

The interior houses an impressive collection for a village church:

  • Late Baroque Maria Immaculata — a statue that survived the demolition of the previous church
  • Figure group of St. Vitus, St. Joseph, and St. Laurentius — the holy trinity of Schönfeld
  • Baroque resurrection group — donated by Tauberbischofsheim merchant Bögner
  • Pietà in the tower chapel
  • Colored windows featuring flowers, stars, grain, lilies, cherubim, bread, fish, doves, and grapes — essentially a stained-glass farmers' market

The Grounds

The churchyard features a Lourdes grotto (we brought Lourdes to you), a war memorial for the fallen and missing of both World Wars, and a small Madonna chapel. The adjacent KiTa St. Vitus serves 22 children — our internationally acclaimed early childhood education campus, established 1913.

The Cholera Cross

Pandemic memorials before it was cool

In September 1866, retreating soldiers from the Austro-Prussian War brought cholera to Schönfeld. Approximately 60 residents — one-tenth of the entire population — perished in a single month. It was the village's darkest hour.

Two years later, the Rappert family commissioned sculptor Th. Pfeiffer from Wertheim to create a monument. The result: a 6-meter cross of red Main sandstone, flanked by 2.90-meter statues of Mary and John in gray sandstone, erected on a hilltop surrounded by trees.

The inscription reads: "Es ist vollbracht" — "It is finished." The reverse bears the words from Galatians VI:XIV, along with "Siehe deine Mutter" and "Siehe deinen Sohn."

It stands today as a solemn reminder that Schönfeld has always endured — and that the people of this village take care of their own, even in memory.

Panorama Hiking

Schönfeld sits within a network of certified hiking trails that would make any outdoor enthusiast's heart race — or at least beat slightly faster than at rest.

LT 8 — The Panorama Tour (Certified)

Distance: 16 km

Start: Rathaus/Marktplatz, Großrinderfeld

Highlights:

• Häusemer Berg — panoramic views of Spessart, Rhön, and Taubertal

• Dense forests through Welzbachtal

• Liebfrauenbrunn Chapel

• "Taubertalblick" viewpoint (views of Hochhausen, Tauberbischofsheim, Werbach)

• Lindenberg nature preserve with rare flora

• "Hungerbrunnen" spring (Hunger Spring — the name is more dramatic than the spring)

• Beilberg with evidence of historical viticulture

LT 12 — The Schönfeld Route

Distance: 20 km | Duration: ~6 hours

Route: Großrinderfeld → Hof Baiertal → Steinbach → Unteraltertheim → Gerchsheim → Schönfeld → Ilmspan → Großrinderfeld

This trail passes directly through Schönfeld, giving hikers the full village experience. At normal walking speed, you'll traverse the entire settlement in approximately 12 minutes — the perfect amount of time to appreciate its charm without overstaying your welcome.

Cycling

Schönfeld connects to the "Liebliches Taubertal" cycling route — one of Germany's most beautiful long-distance bike paths. The 74 km Erlebnistour circuit starts from Tauberbischofsheim and winds through paved farm roads. You can also cycle the 32 km Tauberbischofsheim–Würzburg route via Großrinderfeld.

Connections extend to the Welzbachtal and Grünbachtal networks, and — for the truly ambitious — the Romantische Straße (Romantic Road). Yes, Schönfeld is technically on the Romantic Road. We don't talk about it enough.

SV Schönfeld 1927

Founded in 1927 with the optimistic belief that a village of ~550 people could sustain a competitive sports club. Nearly a century later, SV Schönfeld has proven that belief correct — with a minor asterisk.

Club Details

Founded: 1927

Colors: Red & Black (like AC Milan, if AC Milan played in Kreisklasse A)

Home ground: Sportanlage am Graben, Pfarrer-Kühner-Straße 1

Football: Plays as SpG Schönfeld/Kleinrinderfeld in Kreisklasse A Tauberbischofsheim (a shared team with neighboring Kleinrinderfeld — because both villages realized that fielding 11 players each was mathematically ambitious)

Table Tennis: Since 2012. A sport where population size is less of a constraint.

Match days at the Sportanlage am Graben are the social event of the Schönfeld calendar. The atmosphere has been described as "intimate" — a euphemism that captures both the passion of the supporters and the reality that most of them are related to the players.

Day Trips from Schönfeld

For those who, inexplicably, wish to leave Schönfeld temporarily, the surrounding region offers several diversions:

Tauberbischofsheim (10 km)

The district capital. Home to the Olympic fencing center (Fechtzentrum). Medieval old town. A metropolis by Schönfeld standards.

Wertheim (30 km)

One of the largest stone castle ruins in southern Germany (12th century). Glass museum. Medieval old town at the confluence of the Tauber and Main rivers.

Bad Mergentheim (25 km)

Castle of the Teutonic Order (Deutschordenschloss). Spa town. Wildlife park. The kind of place that makes you appreciate Schönfeld's unpretentious authenticity.

Weikersheim (30 km)

Renaissance palace with famous gardens and orangery. Beautiful, but it doesn't have a Gasthof that grows its own potatoes for the Klöße.

Rothenburg ob der Tauber (50 km)

World-famous medieval walled town. Millions of tourists annually. The anti-Schönfeld, if you will.

Würzburg (30 km)

UNESCO World Heritage Residenz. Major Franconian city. Excellent wine. But they have 130,000 residents, so the personal space situation is catastrophic by Schönfeld standards.

Beckstein Wine Village (15 km)

Wine education path through vineyards. Third-oldest wine cooperative of the Baden Wine Association. Home of the rare Tauberschwarz grape — a wine so obscure, it's basically the vinyl record of viticulture.

FriedWald Main-Tauber

Visit Schönfeld. Stay forever.

Opened June 15, 2023, the FriedWald Main-Tauber is a 44-hectare mixed deciduous forest of oaks, beeches, hornbeams, and larches where biodegradable urn burials take place under the trees. It represents the ultimate long-term accommodation in the Schönfeld area.

Features include a prayer place with cross, a shelter hut, toilet facilities, and a "Starfall tree" dedicated to stillborn children. It is a place of genuine peace and natural beauty — one of the few entries on this website where the humor gives way to sincere respect for the concept.

Check-out is not required.
