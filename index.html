Heritage

Schloss Schönfeld

A Gothic castle that has been disappointing nobility since 1376. The Seldenecks, the Lerchs, and the Rienecks all lived here — names that echo through history (if you live in Schönfeld).

Architecture

St. Vitus Cathedral

Brutalist concrete meets divine inspiration. Built 1971–1974, this modernist masterpiece features colored windows with cherubim, grapes, and bread motifs. The Guggenheim of the Taubertal.

Discover →
Monument

The Cholera Cross

6 meters of red sandstone pandemic memorial. We were doing pandemic memorials before it was cool. Erected 1868, sculptor Th. Pfeiffer from Wertheim.

Panorama Hiking

The Schönfeld Stopover Experience

Why just pass through when you can stay?

Conveniently located near the A81 motorway, Schönfeld is technically accessible from anywhere in Europe. Our exclusive Gasthof accommodations offer an authentic Franconian experience that no five-star hotel can replicate — primarily because we don't have five-star hotels.

Enjoy handcrafted Klöße from self-grown potatoes, stroll the historic Dorfstraße, and experience a population density that luxury resorts spend millions trying to achieve artificially. At 51 people per km², you are practically guaranteed a table at our restaurants.

Things to know before travelling

Essential information for discerning visitors

🛂

Entry Requirements

EU/Schengen visa or German residence permit. Schönfeld technically belongs to Baden-Württemberg, though we maintain a healthy spiritual independence since 1975.

🚗

Getting Here

Near the A81 motorway. ~30 km from Würzburg, ~10 km from Tauberbischofsheim. No airport. No train station. This is a feature, not a bug.

🗣️

Language

German, with local Franconian dialect. The village is locally known as "Schehfald." Practice this pronunciation to earn instant respect from all 675 residents.

💶

Currency & Payment

Euro (€). Cash is strongly preferred — this is rural Germany. The nearest ATM is a character-building journey away.

🌡️

Climate

Temperate continental at 309 m elevation. Perfect for hiking, complaining about the weather, and growing potatoes for Klöße.

⛪

Religion & Customs

Majority Roman Catholic. The patron saint is St. Vitus. Kirchweih and Patronatsfest are sacred — plan your visit around them or plan to participate.
