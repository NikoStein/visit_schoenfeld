Schönfeld Calendar

A year-round festival of culture, tradition, and Franconian Gemütlichkeit

The Schönfeld Festival Year

For a village of 675 residents, Schönfeld maintains a cultural calendar that would exhaust a city ten times its size. From the ancient Patronatsfest to the intimate Weihnachtsmarkt, every season brings its own traditions — each one older than most countries.

All events listed below are free of charge, because in Schönfeld, charging admission to your neighbors would be socially catastrophic.

☀️ Summer Season

June through August — when the beautiful field is at peak beauty

Jun 15
Festival

Patronatsfest St. Vitus

📍 St. Vitus Church & Surroundings

The feast of St. Vitus, patron saint of Schönfeld. Held on the Sunday after June 15, this is the village's premier spiritual and social event. Holy Mass is followed by what can only be described as "aggressive hospitality" — Bratwurst, beer, cake, and the complete inability to leave without being fed at least twice.

Pro tip: Arrive on an empty stomach. Leave regretting nothing.

Free Admission
Jun —
Music

Concert Afternoon at the Dorfgemeinschaftshaus

📍 Dorfgemeinschaftshaus Schönfeld

All three municipal music bands perform in one glorious afternoon. That's three separate musical ensembles for a municipality of ~2,000 people. The per-capita brass-instrument-to-resident ratio in Großrinderfeld is believed to exceed that of New Orleans.

The Musikverein Schönfeld (contact: Matthias Nahm, 09344 95055) leads the charge with a repertoire spanning traditional Blasmusik to... more traditional Blasmusik. But delivered with passion that transcends genre.

Free Admission
Aug 10
Tradition

Kirchweih

📍 Schönfeld Village Center

The annual church dedication festival, held on the Sunday after August 10 (St. Laurentius Day). A tradition that predates the castle, the cholera, and the administrative annexation. Kirchweih is not just an event — it is a statement of cultural continuity.

Expect music, food, community, and the unmistakable feeling that this village has been celebrating in exactly this way for centuries. Because it has.

Free Admission

🍂 Autumn Harvest

September through November — harvest season and the wine gets better

Oct —
Market

Bauernmarkt Großrinderfeld

📍 Großrinderfeld Village Center (~6 km)

Every two years in October, the streets of Großrinderfeld transform into an open-air market of agricultural magnificence. Thousands of visitors descend upon the municipality — temporarily multiplying the population to levels not seen since the 1866 military passage (though with considerably happier outcomes).

What to expect: Homemade bread from old family recipes, organic cheese, local wine, honey, watermelons, potatoes, onions, and cakes that have survived wars, plagues, and administrative reforms with their flavor intact.

Free Admission
Autumn 🍷
Wine

Taubertal Wine Festivals

📍 Various locations across the Taubertal

Autumn is wine season in the Liebliches Taubertal. The 204 km wine road comes alive with cellar tours, tastings, and festivals. Featured varieties include the rare Tauberschwarz — a historic red grape that was nearly extinct before local vintners rescued it, making it the viticultural equivalent of a conservation success story.

Nearby: Beckstein Wine Village (15 km) offers a wine education path through the vineyards. Learn, taste, question your life choices.

Oct —
Gastronomy

Potato Harvest at Gasthof Hahner

📍 Dorfstraße 9, Schönfeld

The annual potato harvest means one thing: peak Klöße season. Gasthof Hahner's self-grown potatoes reach their flavor zenith in autumn, making this the optimal window for experiencing what some have called "the most honest food in Franconia."

Hours: Fri 17–20, Sat–Sun 11–14:30 & 17–20. Closed Mon/Wed/Thu. Plan accordingly.

❄️ Winter Wonderland

December through February — when the silence becomes a feature

Dec 15
Christmas

Schönfeld/Großrinderfeld Weihnachtsmarkt

📍 Between Church & Town Hall, Großrinderfeld

Third Sunday of Advent. The space between church and town hall transforms into a winter wonderland — a term we use without irony. Twinkling lights. The scent of evergreen boughs. Warm Glühwein steaming in the cold air. Christmas melodies from the Musikverein. Handmade gifts from local artisans.

It's like the Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt, except you can actually move, hear yourself think, and the Glühwein vendor knows your name.

Free Admission
Dec —
Christmas

Gerchsheim Weihnachtsmarkt

📍 Gerchsheim (~4 km)

Our sister village's Christmas market — a separate event offering its own distinct character. The 4 km journey from Schönfeld to Gerchsheim through the winter landscape constitutes what we call the "Schönfeld Christmas Market Crawl." Both markets in one weekend is technically possible and culturally encouraged.

Free Admission
All ❄️
Wellness

Extreme Silence Retreat

📍 Literally anywhere in Schönfeld

No booking required. No instructor needed. No app subscription. Simply step outside after 8 PM on any winter evening and experience silence at a depth that meditation retreats charge thousands to approximate.

The only sounds: wind through bare trees, the distant bark of a farm dog, and the faint hum of the A81 reminding you that civilization exists somewhere out there. It does. You just don't need it right now.

Always Free | No Reservation Required

Year-Round Experiences

Some experiences in Schönfeld are not bound by season. They are permanent features of the landscape — available 365 days a year, weather permitting (and sometimes even then).

Match Day at Sportanlage am Graben

When: Football season (check Kreisklasse A Tauberbischofsheim schedule)

Where: Pfarrer-Kühner-Straße 1

Watch SpG Schönfeld/Kleinrinderfeld compete in the Kreisklasse A. The atmosphere is described as "electric" by participants and "a nice outing" by everyone else.

Freiwillige Feuerwehr Open Days

When: Anniversary celebrations and special occasions

Where: Kleinrinderfelder Str. 15

The volunteer fire department (Commander: Mario Körner) celebrates milestones with community events. 80+ years of keeping Schönfeld safe — and throwing excellent parties to celebrate that fact.

Walking the Dorfstraße

When: Any time

Duration: 4–6 minutes end to end (8–12 if you stop to chat, which you will)

The Schönfeld equivalent of the Champs-Élysées. Home to both restaurants, multiple historic buildings, and the concentrated essence of village life. Walk it once for the experience. Walk it twice for the social obligations.
